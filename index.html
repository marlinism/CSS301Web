How to Afford Necessary Technology Devices for Study

How to Afford Necessary Technology Devices for Study

by Tuong Banh

As a result of COVID-19, University of Washington – Bothell campus (UWB) has changed the classes into an online platform. Since then, we received a number of questions from students about the support in technology devices during this pandemic. Despite receiving the funding from the government in order to help students who are having some difficulties in lacking laptops, and Wi-Fi hotspot to study, we are unable to support all students.

Due to COVID-19, many UWB students who do not have technology devices were unable to finish their homework and tests, some of them had to drop classes, or took a gap year. This leads to the delay in some students’ academic plans and in some internship opportunities. Understanding your difficulties, UWB advisors have made some different research on how to support students.

The sections that follow outline multiple solutions that you – UWB students can take to ensure that you will receive the technology support while pursuing the degree in UWB.

Borrow laptops and Wi-Fi hotspot from University of Washington Bothell Information Technology (UWB - IT)

The first option to think of is to complete the form to borrow laptops and Wi-Fi hotspot from University of Washington Bothell Information Technology (UWB – IT).

By following the steps shown on the website, you can complete a form online or schedule an appointment with an advisor who can walk you through the process.

If you do not think that you are able to borrow by applying yourself, you can ask advisors for help. UWB advisors are welcome to help UWB students to get through this pandemic. You can visit this site for the appointments.

Borrow technology devices from UW Student Technology Loan Program

Beside borrowing technology devices from UWB – IT, you can also borrow them from UW Student Technology Loan Program.

Being a UWB student, you have many opportunities to access many publics helps from nonprofit organizations. UW Student Technology Loan Program is also a nonprofit organization that can support you with technology devices. The form is on this website. There are a variety of devices that you can borrow on there, from iPad to MacBook. The application is free, and you can choose the duration of borrowing from a couple of days until a whole quarter.

If you are unable to fill in the form, you can try to give them the call 206-685-6090 in the business days or email them stlp@uw.edu. You can also walk to their offices on the UW Seattle campus. More information is on here .

Pay for technology devices monthly without any interested

If you want to have your own devices, you should think of the program monthly payment without any interest.

Currently, there are many programs that students can split the full amount of the payment and pay for part of it monthly. There is no additional interest, and you can also get the better price when you are able to show the proof of being a student.

If you are unable to pay for the price, you can apply for some jobs on campus. Additionally, the UW online job opportunity database gives students access to campus jobs which can assist in paying tuition; you can browse this database by visiting here.

Conclusion

Students that do not have technology devices can end up failing their classes and must take a gap year. The solutions above require a minimal amount of effort; there is a small amount of additional cost that is needed; they can also be applied by everyone without any requirement. By applying the solutions above, you can be assured to have a laptop while pursuing your degree at UWB.
